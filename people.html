It is an organic winery that uses regenerative and biodynamic practices which are rooted in Rudolf Steiner’s philosophy.

We’re family owned. Nicholas and Pen are married with two children, Sylvia and Arthur, who have grown up at the Home and Netherwood vineyards. Pen and Nicholas own and manage the business. Nicholas looks after our three vineyards and the wine making with his team; Pen manages the business and looks over the restaurant and cellar door with another wonderfulteam of people.

Pen’s parents and siblings have invested in our land and vineyards since the beginning, helping make all of this possible. Pen’s father Rod is a 5th generation horticulturalist from North Otago, (interestingly, Rod’s family grew the first table grapes NZ). Pen’s mum Stacey provides much cheer and family support along with Nicholas’ parents who help out with so much. Jo and Cam, Pen’s siblings are energetic supporters and offer fresh perspectives.

Russell and Kumiko Black planted the original Black Estate vines in 1993, at what is now called the Home Vineyard. We purchased that property from Russell in 2007. We kept the name and are grateful that Russell and Kumiko decided to plants vines on their Omihi hillside.

This dream is only possible with our staff. Somehow, each person has followed a path to be here with us at Black Estate. We’re grateful and learning a lot from each other, and having quite a lot of fun along the way!
